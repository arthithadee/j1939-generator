J1939 Protocol Facts You Might Not Know:

Not Just Trucks: J1939 isn't limited to over-the-road trucks! It's the standard for communications in everything from **marine engines** to massive **agricultural tractors** (like combines) and **construction equipment**.

SAE Standard: J1939 is developed by the **Society of Automotive Engineers (SAE)**. It is essentially the "rule book" for how computers talk to each other in heavy-duty commercial vehicles.

Prioritized Messages: J1939 uses a **message priority system**. Critical messages like "Brake Status" always get transmitted before less critical messages like "Vehicle Identification Number." This ensures safety functions are always prioritized.

PGN Database: There are thousands of defined **Parameter Group Numbers (PGNs)**. These numbers are centrally managed and documented, meaning a PGN for "Engine Speed" means the exact same thing whether it's coming from a Peterbilt or a John Deere machine.
